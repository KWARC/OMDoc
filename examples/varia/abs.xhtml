imports from: arith1, relation1, pl1, pl0, truthval
Lemma

The Absolute Value function is non-negative.

∀

This gives rise to the following Mathematica Code, which will be loaded by Analytica: 

 Unprotect[Abs]; Abs /: (Abs[a_] < 0) := False; Abs /: (0 <= Abs[a_]) := True; Protect[Abs];
Proof

  1. By definition, abs is the identity on the positive real numbers and the negative identity on the negative real numbers.

  2. As both branches are non-negative, we have proven the assertion.
