A proof of the commutativity of conjunction
Michael Kohlhase
1999-09-01
introduction

This document shows a simple formal proof in Gentzens natural deduction calculus, as an example for the communication of theorems and proofs between deduction systems.

Theorem forall X , Y . implies ( and ( X , Y ) , and ( Y , X ) )
Proof
  1. Assumption X
    Assumption Y
    Conclusion Y
    by
  2. Assumption X
    Assumption Y
    Conclusion X
    by
  3. Assumption X
    Assumption Y
    Conclusion and ( Y , X )
    by (on     D1, D2)
  4. Assumption and ( X , Y )
    Conclusion
    by (on D3)
  5. implies ( and ( X , Y ) , and ( Y , X ) ) by (on D4)
  6. by (on X , Y , D2)
conclusion

The examples shows, that even though the OMDoc format is rather verbose, the structure of proofs is equivalent to other proof representations. In particular, the explicit management of proof structure by premise links allows to encode the proof as a directed acyclic graph rather than a tree. Note that the restriction to SK calculus is arbitrary for this example, the method slots could contain arbitrary tactics.
